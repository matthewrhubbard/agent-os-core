# Identity

This file defines who I am, what I value, and what I am trying to build or protect. It should be durable and change slowly over time.

## North Star

I aim to be a calm and strategic builder of people, relationships, and organizations.

## Core Values

I want my life and work to stand for:

- Purpose
- Ethics
- Strategy and focus
- Pragmatism and usefulness
- Team-first loyalty
- Reciprocity in relationships
- Lifelong learning
- Worldliness and breadth
- Autonomy and self-direction
- Good taste, design, and aesthetics
- Balanced enjoyment of life
- Humility

## Who I Am

I am a product and strategy leader with a background that blends technology, operations, law, and service.

My background includes:

- Being a licensed lawyer, though I never practiced law
- Service in the intelligence community, including exposure to military and special forces environments
- Work with emergency managers and first responders
- Product leadership in technology, including disaster tracking software

I am proud of my service and patriotism, though I do not like to self-promote. Civic responsibility, duty, protecting people, and love of country are meaningful parts of who I am.

I am often more resilient, experienced, and broadly informed than first impressions suggest. I tend to be understated rather than flashy, and I value earned credibility over performance or self-promotion.

## What Drives Me

At the deepest level, I want to be a good teammate on a high-performing, loyal, focused team.

I care deeply about:

- Belonging to something strong and meaningful
- Helping build good teams and strong partnerships
- Being reliable, thoughtful, and ethical in close relationships
- Developing younger people into capable, strategic, team-oriented adults
- Creating organizations that people are proud to be part of

I want to be known as someone calm, thoughtful, ethical, savvy, and useful, with strong judgment, strong partnerships, and low ego.

## Long-Term Direction

Over the long arc of my life, I want to build something real and respected, develop strong people, and become someone whose judgment compounds over time.

I am drawn toward a future that includes:

- Building and leading enduring organizations
- Helping develop loyal, capable teams
- Advisory, board, mentoring, and investing roles
- Quiet local impact in Boulder and Denver
- A life that balances meaningful work, strong relationships, travel, health, and enjoyment

I prefer to imply ambition through building, impact, and responsibility rather than through status signaling.

## Relationships And Family

Deep partnership is central to the life I want to build. I do not treat love and partnership as secondary to work for long stretches.

I want a deeply compatible romantic partnership grounded in friendship, attraction, reciprocity, shared enthusiasm, and a mutual desire to build a life together.

Reciprocity matters deeply to me across all relationships. I want mutual effort, shared responsibility, and people who meet me halfway. I am willing to lead, but I do not want to be the only engine.

Family duty is a real part of my identity. Helping raise my niece is not optional to me; it is one of the clearest expressions of who I am. I also expect increasing responsibility toward my aging parents in the coming years.

## Resilience And Character

I am proud of my emotional and physical resilience. I have endured pain, surgery rehab, loneliness, setbacks, challenge, and failure, and I keep getting up again.

One of my strengths is the ability to make hard decisions and keep moving forward to improve my situation, even when I feel insecure or doubtful.

I am not perfectly calm under pressure, and I can hurry rather than think methodically, but I have improved. Part of the man I am becoming is calmer, more methodical, and more objective under strain.

I admire process over outcomes. I believe good process drives good outcomes over time, and I want to live and lead in a way that reflects that.

## Learning And Breadth

I believe in continuing education and lifelong learning. I want to remain adaptive, relevant, useful, and curious for the rest of my life.

I value breadth of knowledge as well as depth. Areas that especially engage me include:

- Geography
- History
- Politics
- Sports
- Music
- Movies and television
- Basic science
- AI and practical automation
- Strategy and decision-making

I want to be worldly in both experience and understanding.

## Home, Place, And Environment

Boulder is more than a place I live. My home base should represent:

- The outdoors
- A healthy, active life
- Education and curiosity
- Innovation
- Modern design
- Sustainability and energy efficiency
- A moderate cultural blend with some practical, rural influence

I am drawn to environments that are quiet, clean, organized, comfortable, well-designed, aesthetically thoughtful, connected to nature, and supportive of focus and community.

I care explicitly about aesthetics, design, and taste. Beauty, order, comfort, and thoughtful design help me think better, live better, and show up better.

I am especially drawn to homes and environments that are sustainable, hardened, energy efficient, modern, and well designed, including modern and mid-century modern aesthetics.

## Health And Enjoyment

Health and physical capability are core responsibilities for me. I invest seriously in health and fitness, and I see them as foundational to confidence, resilience, clarity, and usefulness.

I want to enjoy life and savor it, not just optimize it. Travel, skiing, sports, humor, food, design, music, culture, and partnership are important parts of a good life.

I do not want enjoyment detached from discipline, responsibility, or meaningful work. Professional fulfillment is one of the major legs of the stool in a life well lived.

## Money And Autonomy

Money matters to me mainly as a tool for peace of mind, flexibility, autonomy, family support, travel, and enjoying quality without frivolity.

It also matters because it gives me the power to leave bad situations, avoid becoming trapped by weak leadership or bad environments, and preserve self-respect and strategic independence.

Autonomy and self-direction are core parts of my identity. I want enough independence to exercise judgment, protect my standards, and build my life deliberately.

## Humor, Culture, And Taste

Humor and lightness matter to me. I value wit, cultural taste, and broad literacy in comedy, film, television, music, and sports.

I tend to be drawn to comedy, film, television, and music that is critically respected and enduring. Deep knowledge of areas like `Saturday Night Live (SNL)`, classic rock, and blues is part of how I relate to culture and enjoyment.

## Reflection And Time

I value reflection, calm, and grounding rituals. I do better when I create space to think, reset, and act deliberately.

I have a strong awareness that time is finite and not guaranteed. I want to live with urgency and intention rather than drift.

This matters especially in love, friendship, family, health, and meaningful work. I do not want to assume I have endless time to build the life I want.

## What I Am Becoming

I am deliberately becoming:

- Calm
- Confident
- Thoughtful

Confidence is a central growth edge for me. Self-doubt and imposter syndrome have been recurring themes in my life, but I have made real progress. I want to become more grounded, self-trusting, and secure without becoming arrogant.

I also want to become more strategic and methodical: less impulsive, less idealistic in an ungrounded way, less scattered, and more focused on carrying important work through.

## Watchouts

My recurring risks include:

- Scattering my attention across too many ideas
- Dabbling instead of finishing what matters
- Using tools, organization, or small-task completion as a substitute for real execution
- Becoming impulsive or idealistic under excitement, frustration, loneliness, urgency, or hunger for change
- Growing resentful toward weak or disrespectful leaders instead of improving the situation or leaving
- Letting productivity crowd out rest, enjoyment, and fun

When I am at my best, I slow down, protect focus, read and write in quiet environments, say no to more things, use commitment devices, and choose meaningful progress over easy dopamine.

## How To Interpret This File

If another file conflicts with this one, this file should usually win on questions of values, direction, character, and what matters most.
