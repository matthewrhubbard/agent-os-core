# Meeting Prep

Use this skill to prepare for a meeting so you enter with a clear objective, the right context, and a concrete plan for what success looks like.

## Purpose

This skill helps turn meetings from passive calendar events into intentional working sessions with defined outcomes.

## Context Check

Before using this skill:

- Identify the current project, domain, or life area
- Confirm the meeting type: decision, update, working session, relationship-building, or problem-solving
- Identify the participants and any relevant people files
- Review the most relevant project notes or source-of-truth files
- If key context is missing, ask one targeted question with likely options, brief tradeoffs, and a recommended default, or state assumptions explicitly when reasonable

## When To Use

- Before an important meeting
- When the meeting has unclear purpose or stakes
- When preparing for a stakeholder conversation
- When the meeting could create follow-up or decisions that matter

## Core Principles

- Know what a good outcome looks like
- Prepare only the context that materially changes the discussion
- Tailor prep to the people in the room
- Focus on decisions, risks, and next steps
- Avoid over-preparing low-value meetings

## Workflow

## 1. Define The Objective

Clarify:

- Why this meeting exists
- What outcome would make it successful
- Whether you need a decision, alignment, input, or relationship progress

## 2. Identify The Participants

Note:

- Who will be there
- Their likely priorities
- What each person may care about or resist
- What context they already have or lack

## 3. Gather The Minimum Useful Context

Review:

- Relevant project notes
- Open questions
- Prior decisions
- Risks, dependencies, or recent changes

## 4. Prepare The Discussion Plan

Write down:

- Main talking points
- Questions to ask
- Decisions needed
- Risks or disagreements to surface
- Desired next steps

## Output Structure

1. Meeting objective
2. Participants and stakeholder notes
3. Relevant context
4. Open questions or decisions needed
5. Recommended discussion plan
6. Desired next steps

## Quality Bar

A good meeting brief:

- Makes the purpose of the meeting explicit
- Tailors prep to the participants
- Separates context from noise
- Helps drive a concrete outcome

## Common Failure Modes

- Preparing around topics instead of outcomes
- Ignoring participant dynamics
- Bringing too much context
- Leaving without clear next steps
