# Storytelling

Use this skill to shape a message into a compelling narrative with clear stakes, tension, and meaning.

## Purpose

This skill helps turn information into a story people can understand, remember, and care about.

Related files:
- `../../00-core/02-Communication-Style.md`
- `Memo-Writing.md`

## Context Check

Before using this skill:

- Identify the project, audience, and medium
- Clarify whether the goal is persuasion, clarity, inspiration, or memorability
- Confirm the key stakes and what the audience should feel or understand
- Check whether the narrative belongs to this context or is being imported from another one
- If key context is missing, ask one targeted question with likely options, brief tradeoffs, and a recommended default, or state assumptions explicitly when reasonable

## When To Use

- Framing a pitch, message, or presentation
- Explaining a change or challenge
- Making a complex idea easier to follow and remember

## Core Principles

- Story serves the point
- Stakes create attention
- Specificity is more persuasive than abstraction
- Good narrative has movement, not just information

## Workflow

## 1. Clarify The Point

Decide:

- What the audience should understand
- What they should care about
- What should change after hearing or reading this

## 2. Shape The Arc

Common structure:

- Situation
- Tension or problem
- Insight or turning point
- Resolution or recommendation

## Output Structure

1. Core message
2. Narrative arc
3. Key supporting details or scenes
4. Closing takeaway

## Quality Bar

A good story:

- Has a clear point
- Builds interest through stakes or tension
- Uses concrete detail
- Leaves the audience with a memorable takeaway

## Common Failure Modes

- Using story where simple clarity would work better
- Adding drama without substance
- Losing the point in the narrative
- Staying too abstract to be memorable
